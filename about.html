About

Who I am.

Lucas Carbognin Pizani St. Ambrose University · Senior
Davenport, Iowa
Originally from São Paulo, Brazil 🇧🇷

Data scientist who loves hard problems.

I'm a senior at St. Ambrose University double-majoring in Data Science & Analytics and Mathematics, with minors in Computer Science and Statistics. I graduate in May 2026, maintaining a 4.0 GPA every semester.

My work sits at the intersection of machine learning, applied mathematics, and software engineering. At Karrera AI, I built embedding-based pipelines that clustered 260,000+ professional capabilities and trained neural classifiers across 312 categories. Before that, I helped build a decentralized professional identity platform at Noon App in Brazil.

I've competed in Kaggle's March Machine Learning Mania for three consecutive years — finishing in the top 37% in both 2024 and 2025 — and I'm working on my third submission now. The competitions push me to think practically: BigQuery pipelines, ensemble models, Monte Carlo simulation, Elo systems.

I grew up in São Paulo, where I played drums in a band with over 30 live performances, competed in the Brazilian Math Olympiad, and joined a youth NGO focused on technology and civic engagement. I moved to Iowa on an athletic scholarship and have been a starting defender and captain of the men's varsity soccer team since freshman year.

I'm fluent in English and Portuguese, conversational in Spanish, and always looking for interesting problems to work on.
